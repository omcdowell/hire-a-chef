Where we'd find our chef

Plenty of real routes -- from a properly trained pro to a trusted referral. Roughly best-quality first.

Culinary schools

Culinary school job boards

Schools like CCA Manila and Enderun train and place graduates, and CCA runs a public jobs board where we can post a private-chef role straight to their alumni network. The best route to a genuinely trained chef.

CostFree to post
Staff agencies

Licensed staff agencies

DOLE-licensed agencies (e.g. MaidProvider.ph) recruit, NBI-vet and medically screen cooks, place across Makati & BGC, and usually offer a replacement guarantee. The safest, most hands-off option.

CostPlacement fee ≈ ₱20,000 (~1 month's pay)
Job platforms

Online job boards

Post a "private chef for a family" listing on JobStreet, Kalibrr or Indeed PH and screen applicants ourselves. Cheaper than an agency -- but we do the interviewing.

CostIndeed free · Kalibrr/JobStreet ~₱8,000+/post
Facebook groups

Facebook hiring groups

Groups like "Now Hiring Kasambahay" get replies the same day. Fast and free -- but candidates are unscreened, so check references and meet in person first.

CostFree to post
Referrals

Word of mouth

The most trusted local route: ask friends, neighbours and househelp who they'd vouch for. The best cooks usually come recommended rather than advertised.

CostFree (a small thank-you is customary)
Expat networks

Condo & expat networks

Building concierge boards and expat communities constantly circulate trusted cooks moving between households -- a great way to pick up an already-vetted chef.

CostFree
What we're actually buying

It isn't really about the money. It's about what every evening turns into.

01

You walk in, and dinner's already happening

No "what do we even eat tonight." You come home from work to a kitchen that smells incredible and a table being set.

02

Nobody cooks. Nobody washes up.

The thing that quietly ruins weeknights -- gone. The chef shops, cooks, plates, and leaves the kitchen spotless. We just show up and eat.

03

Tell them what you're craving

Sunday roast one night, sinigang the next, handmade pasta on Thursday. We write the menu; they make it sing. Allergies and fussiness welcome.

04

The market run just... happens

Fresh from the Salcedo Saturday market and the palengke, picked by someone who knows what's good that day. No more sad supermarket trolley at 9pm.
